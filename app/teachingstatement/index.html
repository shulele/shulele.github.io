Teaching Statement

Feb 1, 2019 teaching

I love to train the next generation of hydrologists and environmental scientists. It is my belief that every student is capable of tasting the passion that I feel for hydrologic modeling by becoming a practitioner in the exploration of hydrological theories, researches and models. I had three undergraduate sessions of teaching on Geography Information System years ago. My teaching philosophy rests on three tenets: simplifying the complexity with mathematics, realizing ideas with programming, and integrating students in research.

Simplifying the complexity with mathematics. I invite my students to practice a series of steps: describing a natural problem, using physical law, making assumptions, making mathematical statement of the laws and solving math equations finally. As the fundamental procedure to think and solve the complex scientific problems, I will ask my students to practice and master this skill.

Realizing ideas with programming. We all have more ideas than solutions. Programming is one of the best options to realize or visualize your ideas about modeling, improvement and demonstration. I teach students the steps and tips in computing algorithm as well as basic data structure, management, analysis and visualization in various programming language. For example, most of beginners are opt to use GUI tools to process and analysis spatial data processing and analysis, such as ArcGIS and QGIS. Actually, doing such tasks in programming, e.g. Matlab, R or Python, are so efficient that does not further understanding of theory and data only, but also inspires new ideas. After my class, the programming will be student’s first option to do complex computing or data processing.

Integrating students in research. Learning from practices is always better than listening in classroom, so I design the scientific problems in researches as on-class examples, solvable homework and projects for students. Due to compatibility of modeling methods, the problems from other fields are very welcomed in my class. For example, in Numeric Methods in Geosciences, one of class I plan to teach, the numeric methods can be used for various fields and topics, such as hydrology, mechanics, geology, limnology, etc., so students must think and practice the scientific problems in their own researches that fits the numeric methods discussed in class. In the final projects, students must pick a best-fit numeric method to solve doable problems in their fields. Of course, I will help them to define and solve the problems with tenet one and two mentioned above.

I find teaching students fulfilling because it offers an opportunity for me to engage and collaborate with the next generation of scholars and leaders. Besides, I also learn from my students. I offer a range of classes, from technical courses on hydrologic modeling, spatial data processing, data mining to seminars on urbanization and climate change.
